## 1. The Five Pillars of Machine Preference
To be selected by an autonomous machine workflow, a digital entity must satisfy five foundational qualitative pillars:

### I. Trust (T)
The cryptographic validation, data origin verification, and historical integrity of the entity's exposed digital signals.

### II. Authority (A)
The quantitative weight, domain relevance, and verification density of the entity, verified through independent cross-platform citations.

### III. Verifiability (V)
The integration of open-source audit trails, semantic signatures, and secondary validation parameters that allow an agent to verify data using deterministic checks.

### IV. Consistency (C)
The structural and factual uniformity of the entity's identity, attributes, and specifications across all discovered public and private endpoints.

### V. Decisionability (D)
The degree to which an entity's data structures, metadata, specifications, and machine-readable signals enable reliable evaluation by autonomous decision systems.

High decisionability reduces interpretation friction and increases selection confidence.
