## 1. The Probabilistic Interpretation Problem

Modern language models operate probabilistically and may produce different outputs when presented with identical inputs. While this behaviour is valuable for reasoning, creativity, and language generation, autonomous execution systems require additional deterministic validation layers when making operational decisions.

Decisionability therefore focuses on reducing interpretation variance by increasing structural clarity, machine readability, and verification density.

## 2. Rule-Based Data Infrastructure Requirements

To achieve high-confidence decisionability, digital entities must serve data through deterministic, rule-based infrastructure. This model mandates:

* **Strict Schema Adherence:** All transactional and organizational data must map to immutable schema protocols to prevent parsing failure.
* **Algorithmic Guardrails:** Data must be structured to pass hard-coded verification filters rather than relying on natural language interpretation.
* **Zero Ambiguity Tolerance:** Content must be optimized to eliminate processing friction and interpretation variance during automated evaluation cycles.
